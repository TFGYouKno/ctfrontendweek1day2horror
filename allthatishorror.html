About

Horror is a genre of speculative fiction that is intended to frighten, scare, or disgust. It creates an eerie and frightening atmosphere to evoke emotional responses such as fear and anxiety in the audience. Horror can be supernatural or non-supernatural, with elements of the unknown, the mysterious, and the macabre.

Movies

Classic Horror Films

Classic horror films are those that have stood the test of time and continue to be celebrated for their impact on the genre. Examples include Psycho, The Exorcist, and Night of the Living Dead.

Modern Horror Films

Modern horror films explore contemporary fears and anxieties, often incorporating social commentary and innovative storytelling techniques. Examples include Get Out, Hereditary, and A Quiet Place.

Subgenres

Psychological Horror

Psychological horror focuses on the mental and emotional aspects of fear, often delving into the psyche of the characters and the audience. Examples include Black Swan and Shutter Island.

Slasher Films

Slasher films are known for their graphic violence and suspenseful chase sequences, often featuring a masked killer stalking and murdering victims. Examples include Halloween, Friday the 13th, Nightmare on Elm Street, and Scream. There is significant overlap between the classic and slasher genre, with Psycho being a prime example.

Games

Survival Horror

Survival horror games emphasize resource management, puzzle-solving, and atmospheric tension to create a sense of vulnerability and fear in the player. Examples include The Amnesia Series, Resident Evil, and Dead Space.
